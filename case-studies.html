IBM Enterprise License Agreement Renewal

From a $95M renewal proposal to $70M in final negotiated value.

Large enterprise banking environment · Multi-year IBM software estate with complex licensing, compliance, and growth requirements;A

Situation

IBM represented one of the Bank’s largest software investment categories, with fragmented ownership and limited visibility across business units, infrastructure, and applications. The vendor’s renewal proposal of USD 95M–97M, driven by growth assumptions, compliance risks, and overprovisioning, threatened significant overspend and CAPEX exposure without a structured optimization and governance initiative..

Action

A structured multi-phase optimization and governance program launched in October 2024 validated actual software usage, optimized licensing demand, and strengthened the Bank’s negotiation position. Through ELP assessments, automated entitlement reconciliation, AI-driven analytics, and stakeholder governance, we eliminated redundant scope, rationalized demand, and built a defensible optimization baseline that reduced compliance gaps and accelerated commercial decision-making.

Result

Through structured optimization cycles and targeted negotiations, the Bank reduced renewal exposure and secured long-term scalability with compliance assurance. The final 3‑year IBM ELA closed at USD 70.2M, delivering USD 34.8M+ in total financial benefit while strengthening governance, audit readiness, and future growth visibility.

Red Hat Enterprise SaaS Optimisation

From $102M forecast to $60M reality

Global enterprise · Transition from unlimited usage subscription to optimized licensing model

Situation

Operating under a global Enterprise Subscription Agreement, the client faced limited visibility into actual Red Hat product consumption and dependency. Forecasts projected $102M in future subscription costs, while critical workloads still ran on unsupported RHEL 7 without Extended Life Support, creating major operational risk.

Action

We conducted a comprehensive estate assessment, mapping infrastructure dependency against entitlement usage, identifying optimization opportunities, and forecasting validated subscription demand. Lifecycle governance analysis exposed unsupported RHEL 7 deployments, leading to upgrade and remediation recommendations.

Result

Projected subscription requirements were reduced from ~$102M to ~$60M, mitigating ~$10M in operational risk exposure. The client transitioned away from the Enterprise Subscription Program with a commercially sustainable model, improved visibility, and strengthened governance for future renewals.

Hardware Asset Management

From fragmented asset tracking to a fully automated HAM ecosystem across 5,000+ locations.

Global fast-food enterprise · Distributed retail infrastructure with high-volume endpoint dependency

Situation

The client struggled with fragmented hardware asset tracking across thousands of restaurant locations, resulting in duplicate procurement, untracked devices, inconsistent CMDB records, and avoidable hardware replacement costs. Internal teams lacked real-time visibility into asset movement, warranty status, and lifecycle ownership, creating operational inefficiencies at scale.

Action

Proteam implemented an automation-driven HAM framework integrated with ServiceNow CMDB, enabling real-time asset reconciliation, lifecycle tracking, and intelligent reporting across distributed environments. AI-assisted validation workflows identified warranty recovery opportunities, duplicate asset requests, and inactive devices before replacement decisions were made. Automated discovery reconciliation significantly reduced manual effort while improving audit readiness and operational accuracy.

Result

The engagement delivered over $75M in annualised savings through smarter asset utilisation, warranty validation, and procurement optimisation. Automated reporting improved hardware accountability across franchise locations, reduced unnecessary purchases, and enabled faster decision-making through clean, accurate asset intelligence. The client gained a scalable HAM operation aligned to long-term governance and operational efficiency goals.

Oracle on Azure · FinOps + Licensing

Oracle-to-Azure migration cost lever, sized correctly the first time.

Enterprise client · Hybrid Oracle estate migrating to Azure · combined FinOps and licensing exposure

Situation

The client was migrating Oracle workloads to Azure under pressure to reduce cloud spend. Done right, this was a major cost lever — done wrong, an audit risk. Their internal FinOps team didn't have the Oracle licensing depth to size the move defensibly, and their licensing team didn't have the cloud cost model.

Action

We engaged with both lenses on the same engagement — modelling licence positioning under Oracle's cloud licensing policy alongside the Azure cost optimisation. Reserved instance and commitment strategy was sized against the validated licensing footprint, not the gross utilisation curve.

Result

Optimisable cloud spend was reduced by 20–30% in the first 12 months. Critically, the saving was captured without opening audit exposure on the underlying Oracle estate — a combination few consultancies can deliver on a single engagement.

AWS Savings Plan Restructure

One helpdesk question, $3.1M back over three years.

US-headquartered SaaS company · $24M AWS Savings Plan renewal · multi-region production estate

Situation

The client used the cloud cost helpdesk for what felt like a routine question — should they renew their $24M AWS Savings Plan on the same terms? Their internal team's instinct was to renew like-for-like. They wanted a sanity check before committing.

Action

Our helpdesk team modelled the actual usage curve against the existing commitment, identified the over-commitment pattern, and recommended a restructured portfolio combining shorter-term Savings Plans with on-demand for variable workloads. The analysis took less than two weeks.

Result

$3.1M saved over the new three-year term — from one helpdesk query. The client now routes all major cloud commitment decisions through the helpdesk before signing.

ITAM — Snow
Migration duration
6 months
Snow Licence Manager retired in six months — with no operational period of impaired licence visibility.
Snow Licence Manager → Flexera One Migration

Snow → Flexera One migration with zero loss of licence visibility.

Multinational consumer goods client · Post-acquisition consolidation onto Flexera One · sequential publisher cut-over

Situation

Following Snow's acquisition, the client decided to consolidate onto Flexera One. The internal concern was a worst-case migration scenario — an operational period during which the SAM team would be without usable licence positions during a critical contract cycle.

Action

We designed a parallel-run migration with sequential publisher cut-over rather than a single big-bang cutover. Each publisher's data, rules, and reconciliation logic was validated on the new platform before retiring the equivalent Snow workflow — preserving operational continuity throughout.

Result

Six months later, the legacy Snow environment was retired — with no operational period of impaired licence visibility. The SAM team kept full coverage across the migration window and inherited a stronger Flexera One foundation than a rushed cutover would have produced.
