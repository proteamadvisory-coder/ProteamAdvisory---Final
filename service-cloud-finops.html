The problem we solve

FinOps meets SAM. That seam is where money hides.

The basic FinOps motions — tagging, allocation, rightsizing, commitment planning — are well documented and a healthy ecosystem of tools exists around them. What remains scarce is specialist judgement at the points where cloud cost meets traditional software licensing. That's where most enterprises lose money and don't realise it.

BYOL economics. Bringing existing Oracle, Microsoft, IBM, or SAP licences to AWS or Azure is rarely as simple as the marketing implies. Done right, BYOL is a major cost lever. Done wrong, it becomes an audit risk.

Hybrid licensing. Workloads spanning on-premise and cloud — common in Oracle, SAP, and Microsoft estates — create complexity neither pure-FinOps tools nor pure-SAM tools handle well. The optimisation requires both lenses simultaneously.

Commitment planning. Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, and Committed Use Discounts are powerful but inflexible. Forecasting commitment levels needs visibility into both consumption trends AND software licensing constraints.

What's included

Six work-streams. One outcome.

Every licence optimisation engagement covers these areas — adapted in scale and depth to your environment, contracts, and timeline.

— 01

Cloud cost visibility & tagging

Foundation work: ensuring every cloud dollar is allocated to a business owner, environment, and workload. Tag policy design, enforcement, allocation reporting, and showback/chargeback frameworks for finance.

— 02

Rightsizing analysis

Per-workload analysis of compute, storage, and database sizing against actual utilisation. Idle resource ID, over-provisioning correction, and rightsizing recommendations sequenced by savings impact and migration risk.

— 03

Commitment planning

Strategy for Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, and Committed Use Discounts — accounting for consumption forecasts AND software licensing migration plans. Designed to capture savings without locking in commitments that conflict with the broader software estate.

— 04

BYOL & hybrid licensing optimisation

Specialist analysis of how existing Oracle, Microsoft, SAP, IBM licences can be deployed cost-optimally across cloud environments. The audit-risk side matters as much as the cost side — we treat them together.

— 05

Workload placement & architecture review

Where workloads are running, where they should be running, and what the licensing and cost implications of different placement decisions look like. Includes container cost analysis and Kubernetes-aware optimisation.

— 06

Ongoing FinOps operating model

Monthly cost reviews, quarterly commitment refresh, annual architecture review. Optionally embedded as managed service alongside SaaS Management and traditional SAM, giving the CFO a single quarterly view across the entire software and cloud estate.

How we work

A four-stage delivery model. Built around your cloud estate.

Cloud FinOps engagements run 12–24 weeks for an initial programme, then ongoing managed service if elected. Built around AWS, Azure, GCP — and your hybrid licensing.

01

Discover

Free consultation to scope the cloud estate, understand the existing FinOps maturity, and identify the highest-value optimisation surfaces — particularly at the BYOL/hybrid-licensing boundary.

02

Visibility & analysis

Tagging review, cost allocation, rightsizing analysis, BYOL audit. Typically 6–10 weeks. By the end, the CFO has a complete picture of cloud spend, with every dollar allocated and every optimisation surface quantified.

03

Optimise

Execution: rightsizing, commitment planning, BYOL migration, hybrid licensing rationalisation. Sequenced over 12–20 weeks for major estates. Each move tracked back to the savings target.

04

Sustain

Monthly cost optimisation cadence, quarterly business reviews, ongoing licensing-aware FinOps support. Operates alongside traditional SAM and SaaS Management for an integrated view of total software cost.
