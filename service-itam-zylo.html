The problem we solve

SaaS is now 30–50% of enterprise software spend. And mostly invisible.

SaaS spend has grown faster than any other software category for five years running — and is now 30–50% of total enterprise software cost in most organisations. The procurement processes, governance, and SAM discipline that took two decades to mature around traditional licensing have rarely caught up.

Zylo solves the visibility problem. The platform discovers SaaS applications through expense data, SSO, finance feeds, and direct API integration with major vendors. It produces an inventory of what you're actually paying for, who's using it, and when it renews. For most enterprises, the day Zylo goes live is the first day they have honest SaaS visibility.

Visibility alone doesn't change behaviour. The savings come from what you do next — renewal preparation that starts six months early, dormant licence harvesting, application consolidation, structured application ownership, and a renewal workflow that prevents auto-renewal of unused contracts. Most failed Zylo deployments fail at that next step. We focus the engagement on what produces savings, not just dashboards.

What's included

Six workstreams. Visibility plus the operating model to use it.

Whether you're standing up Zylo for the first time or extending an existing deployment — engagements cover these areas.

// 01

Discovery source configuration

Configuration of every discovery source — expense data feeds, SSO integration, finance ERP connection, direct API integrations with major SaaS vendors. The more sources, the more accurate the inventory.

// 02

Application inventory baseline

Producing the first trustworthy inventory of every SaaS application in the estate — including shadow IT, departmental purchases on credit cards, and dormant contracts that nobody admits to owning.

// 03

Application owner programme

Designing the ownership model — who owns Salesforce, who owns ServiceNow, who owns Slack. Clear responsibility for renewal decisions, usage reviews, and licence right-sizing. Without this, inventory data has no executive home.

// 04

Renewal workflow design

Building the renewal workflow in Zylo — alerts at 9, 6, and 3 months before renewal, automatic usage data attached, decision routing through finance and IT, and structured exit gates for renewals that fail justification.

// 05

Integration with broader SAM stack

Connecting Zylo into your wider SAM and procurement environment — ServiceNow, Flexera One, Coupa, SAP Ariba. So SaaS data flows into the wider governance picture instead of living in its own dashboard.

// 06

Savings programme launch

Translating the inventory into a structured savings programme — dormant licence harvesting, application consolidation candidates, renewal targets, and shadow-IT remediation. With a quarterly governance forum to track delivery.
