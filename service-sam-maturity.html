The problem we solve

Your SAM practice has a ceiling. You can feel it.

Every SAM team eventually hits a wall. The savings get harder to find. The same vendors keep generating the same risks. The tool deployment that was supposed to fix everything still requires three analysts to keep it producing usable data. The board wants a single number for software cost and risk — and nobody can produce it without two weeks of manual reconciliation.

That ceiling has a shape, and once you can see it, you can dismantle it. Maturity assessment is how we make the shape visible: where the practice is leaking, where it’s mature but isolated, and where it’s mature on paper but failing in execution.

The output is a sequenced transformation roadmap — not a slide deck. Each capability gap has a defined owner, dependency map, business case, and 12–18 month timeline. The goal is the next maturity level, not the perfect one.

The five dimensions

Where we look. And how we score it.

Every dimension is scored 1–5 against the ISO/IEC 19770 framework, with evidence captured from interviews, tool exports, and process walkthroughs. The shaded score on each card is the industry median — where most enterprises actually sit today.

Dimension 01

People

Roles, skills, span of control, and the operating relationships between SAM, procurement, IT, and finance. Where the function reports, and how senior the owner is.

Dimension 02

Process

Documented workflows for request, approval, deployment, true-up, renewal, and retirement. The extent to which the right things happen by default rather than by individual heroics.

Industry median2 / 5
Dimension 03

Tooling

The SAM platform stack — ServiceNow, Flexera, Snow, Zylo, Aspera — and its actual operating state. Recognition coverage, data freshness, connector health, and the gap between “deployed” and “trusted”.

Industry median3 / 5
Dimension 04

Data quality

Entitlement records, deployment data, consumption telemetry, and the contractual ground truth behind them. Whether the numbers in last quarter’s report would survive a vendor audit.

Industry median2 / 5
Dimension 05

Outcomes

Measurable business value — savings delivered, risk reduced, decisions accelerated. Whether the SAM function can articulate, in numbers a CFO trusts, what it returned this year.

The maturity scale. Five levels.
ISO/IEC 19770 · aligned
LEVEL 01
Ad‑hoc
Reactive. Spreadsheet-driven. The function exists because someone insisted it should.
LEVEL 02
Reactive
Audits and renewals drive the calendar. Tooling deployed but data isn’t trusted yet.
LEVEL 03
Defined
Documented processes, owned roles, repeatable cycles. Savings start to compound.
LEVEL 04
Managed
Cross-functional integration. Decisions are evidence-led. Outcomes reported quarterly.
LEVEL 05
Optimised
Continuous optimisation. Predictive. The SAM function shapes IT strategy, not the reverse.
